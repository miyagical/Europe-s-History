All these newly independent states, many of them radically anti- Western, became members of the United Nations, thus shifting the bal- ance of power in that organization and altering the dynamics of inter- national politics. The Third World, those countries that adhered neither to the Western democratic capitalist states (the First World) nor to the communist bloc (Second World), became a pivotal arena in the Cold War struggles between East and West.
