Marx, Marxism, and Socialism 61 the specter: Pope and Tsar, Metternich and Guizot, French Radicals and German police-spies.” Here, Marx and Engels invoke the bastions of conservatism and the old order in Europe—the Holy Alliance, the pope, the Russian tsar, and the conservative prime ministers of Austria and France—as well as their noncommunist rivals on the Left, the French radicals. However, in 1848, it was revolution, rather than communism, that haunted Europe, and French statesman François Guizot and the Habsburg dynasty’s Metternich were among the first to be swept out of office in that year.
