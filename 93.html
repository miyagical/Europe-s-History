As these traits are passed on from one generation to the next, they become predominant in that population. Those individuals possessing these advantageous characteristics survive and reproduce; those that do not possess them are more likely to perish and eventually to disappear from the population altogether. Darwin’s theories challenged both contemporary science and religion. Although other biologists had pointed to competition and struggle be- tween different species, Darwin focused on the struggle for survival and competition within a single species. It was this, of course, that ac- counted for the changing nature of the species.
